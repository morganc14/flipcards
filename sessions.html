  • Session 1: The Wizard, In the Court, With the Candlestick

    A time of peace has come to Amber, and in the absence of enemies and threats, King Oberon has declared the Prosperity Court and strongly encouraged his children to return from their various pursuits to join the celebration.

    While few of his children are genuinely enthusiastic to celebrate what Liabelle describes as "celebrating murdering everyone", none of them are particularly willing to snub their father without a very good reason, at least not deliberately.

    Liabelle is, of course, in the thich of events, taking advantage of the court to wheel and deal. Cethlenn, still freshly past the death of her doppelganger, is still feeling out how things have unfolded in her absence. Borel rolls in on the heels of rather destructively dealing with an abyssal incursion in Montevalno. Borel's arrival is more good luck than intent, as Ramirez - the king's favorite messanger falcon - never reached him.

    The cabal takes the opportunity to meet at Borel's chicken & waffle restaurant in the city, to catch up and compare notes. While doing so, they spot Bevin coming up the throughfare, with the target of the forthcoming hunt under a tarp.

    Events culminate with a family dinner and Oberon's announcement of said hunt on the morrow, including the expectation that hunting will be done in pairs.

  • Session 2: The Great Hunt

    In the morning, too early for many, the hunt is gathered in an area of thick forest not far from the ocean. Some of the notably pairings include:

    • Cethlenn & Suil Lir - Relying on their shared cunning and Suil Lir's hounds.
    • Liabells & Roichard - Looking to employe some "special amrnament" that Roichard has provided.
    • Borel & Tribute - Tribute is a peacock who Borel rescued from the court. It had been part of a set, and its companion had the bad fortune of irking his majesty, to predictable effect.
    • Diolinda & Fenella - Diolinda is here ot put on a good show, and Fenella is just trying to get through.
    • Oberon is his own team. To make it fair.

    A few others watched, but did not participate.

    Things took a turn when it was revealed that the beast Bevin had brought was not a dragon, but was in fact a basilisk. This resulted in a lot of changing of tactics and the withdrawal of the hounds, and, in general, a lot of chaos.

    In the end, it was Borel who took down the beast, reveallign he had been feeding Tribute a steady supply of his own blood, transforming the creature into a firebird, which carried the basilisk off a cliff. Borel was largely embarassed.
